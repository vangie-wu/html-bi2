Bi-2

Bi-2 (Russian: Би-2 Bi-dva) is a Belarussian alternative rock band, formed in the 1980s in Bobruisk, Belarus. It was one of the most successful with many sales and chart-hits in Russia. Bi-2 was awarded MTV Russian Music Awards for Best Rock Act in 2007.

History

  • The band was formed in 1985 in Minsk, Belarus, by two teenagers, Aleksandr "Shura" Uman and Egor "Lyova" Bortnik.
  • In 1997 they recorded their first album "Bespolaya i Grustnaya Lubov".
  • In 1999 Bi-2 moved to Russia, but their plans were ruined by the Russian financial crisis of 1998,
  • The band went up to popularity only in 2000, recording the score for popular Brother 2 movie by Aleksei Balabanov.
  • Bi-2 continued with "Meow Kiss Me" (2001) and "Inomarki" (2004), both sold gold in Russia.
  • Videos for Bi-2 songs often appear on MTV Russia. In 2005 the double CD "Nechetny Voin" was released, which consists completely of duets and collaborations.
